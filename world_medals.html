Worldwide Medals

Visualisation World Map Medal Distribution by Country

Running has been part of the Olympic Games since they were first held in 1896. The competition programme, inspired by the ancient Greek Olympic Games, includes endurance, relay and hurdle races. The United States has been a dominant nation in these competitions, regularly ranking as the top nation in many editions of the Games.

Women's participation in running events has progressed over the years. Women first took part in the Olympic Games in 1900, but their participation was limited. It was only more than seventy years later, in 1972, that women were allowed to take part in the 1500 metre race. This marked an important step towards gender equality in athletics.

You can discover the history of the medal-winning countries in the 1500m discipline, as well as the names of the winners! There's also an interactive map showing the performances of countries in the various running disciplines. The participation of nations in the running disciplines varies considerably.
